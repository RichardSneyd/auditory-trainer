About NeuroTunetm

NeuroTunetm is a free, dynamic auditory intergration tool! Just select your prefered music file from your device - we recommend complex, low to mid tempo music such as classical or jazz, but it's your choice! Use headphones (bone conduction preferred) for the most potent effects.

I developed NeuroTunetm as a tool for my son's home intervention program, and decided to share it as a free alternative to expensive premium programs such as The Tomatis Method, Auditory Integration Training, brain entrainment programs and so forth. This way, everyone can have access a high quality, innovative auditory training & therapy tool.

NeuroTunetm trains the auditory system by dynamically modulating the playback of music of your choice in ways that stimulate interhemispheric communication, and continuously 'surprise' and challenge the brain. Frequencies in the higher end of the human vocal range are emphasised, thus training the brain to focus on 'signal', while pushing lower frequencies, such as those < 200 Hz into the background (though this is customisable).

Additionally, dynamic binaural beat layering is used to increase brainwave interhemispheric synchronization and to entrain brainwaves in the delta - beta range (0.4-30Hz). Over time this tends to promote brain states conducive to calmness and self-regulation. Sustained use of NeuroTunetm can enhance auditory attention and focus, improving discriminiation and processing, and the ability to filter out noise from signal. As a result, listening becomes more effortless and fluid.

Features

  • Filter Frequency Range: Allows you to set a minimum and maximum frequency range in Hertz (Hz). Dynamic high-pass filtering is then applied within these parameters.
  • Gating Frequency Range: Enables you to set the minimum and maximum gating frequency (how often gating events occur) in seconds, adjusting how the sound is processed over time.
  • Volume Control: Gives you the ability to control the maximum volume through a slider, ensuring that volume modulation never goes above the level you specify.
  • On/Off Toggles for All the Aboce: These toggles let you enable or disable dynamic filtering and gating, dynamic binaural beat layering as well as dynamic playback speed, offering further customization to your auditory training. High-pass filtering and gating are on by default.

Usage

  1. Upload an audio file, select one from the library, or just play the default track to begin.
  2. Customize your experience using the Dynamic Settings.
  3. Start your auditory training by playing the audio.
  4. Shuffle is enabled by default, and will randomly select the next track from the sample library provided.

Purpose and Benefits

NeuroTunetm is designed to be customisable and versatile in operation and application, so while I originally designed it for my son's intervention program for Auditory Processing Disorder (APD), it can be of useful for anyone interested in sharpening their auditory processing skills.

The Theoretical Model

Auditory Filters and Frequency Emphasis

Our technology employs specialized filtering algorithms to emphasize certain frequencies while dampening others. This draws from the "cocktail party effect" in auditory scene analysis (Bregman, Albert S. "Auditory scene analysis: The perceptual organization of sound." MIT press, 1994), focusing the brain's auditory attention on 'signal' over 'noise.'

Interhemispheric Communication

The tool uses the dynamic modulation of sound to engage both hemispheres of the brain, promoting interhemispheric communication. This is grounded in research suggesting the involvement of both hemispheres in complex auditory tasks (Rogalsky, Corianne, et al. "Functional anatomy of language and music perception: Temporal and structural factors investigated using functional magnetic resonance imaging." The Journal of Neuroscience 31.10 (2011): 3843-3852).

Randomized Gating and Neuronal Engagement

By incorporating elements of unpredictability via randomized gating, NeuroTuneâ„¢ stimulates a broader range of auditory neurons. This is consistent with the principle of neural plasticity and the impact of novelty on learning (Buonomano, Dean V., and Michael M. Merzenich. "Cortical plasticity: From synapses to maps." Annual review of neuroscience 21.1 (1998): 149-186).

Dynamic Binaural Beat Layering

NeuroTuneâ„¢ also incorporates a dynamic binaural beat system that allows for real-time manipulation of sound frequencies delivered separately to each ear. This technology is rooted in the binaural beat phenomenon, wherein two slightly different frequencies are played in each ear, and the brain perceives a third tone that is the mathematical difference between the two. This has been studied for its potential effects on cognitive functions, stress levels, and even neural synchronization (Oster, Gerald. "Auditory beats in the brain." Scientific American 229 (1973): 94-102).

The binaural beat system in NeuroTuneâ„¢ is dynamic, sychronizing mathematically with the other filters, meaning the beat frequencies adapt in real-time, thereby providing a richer and more complex auditory journey. This feature helps to engage different brainwave states, optimize focused attention, and enhance cognitive functions.

Customization and Individual Differences

Auditory training programs have traditionally been based around a narrow set of classical music tracks, largely due the residual belief in the 'mozart effect'. However, modern research shows that, while classical music can be very benefitial, there is also great value in utilising music that is to the individuals taste, whatever the genre. By self-selecting the music tracks used, you get automatic boosts in attention/engagement, essentially for free. Not tying strictly to classical music also allows for far greater variation in the intervention, resulting in greater novelty, which in turn drives neuroplastic changes.
